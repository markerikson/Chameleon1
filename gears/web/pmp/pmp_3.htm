3.0 Managerial Process

3.1 Management Objectives and Priorities

The team's main managerial goal is to minimize management overhead for the Chameleon project.  Overhead such as scheduling, task tracking, and customer relations.  Our ultimate goal, of course, is to provide a completed product by the end of the 2003-2004 school year.

3.2 Assumptions, Dependencies, and Constraints

The team is constrained by the fact that we are all students, there are only three of us on the team, and our lack of project experience.

3.3 Risk Management

We plan to have two weekly meetings: a 1-hour meeting on Thursdays for serious project discussion, and a quick 15-minute meeting on Sundays for brief status updates.  If necessary, we may alter our initial meeting plans to handle further discussion.  The meetings will help us stay on top of project issues as they arise.

Meetings and other discussions will handle problems such as miscommunication.  Should we run into technical difficulties or a task we've underestimated, we will most likely delay the relevant feature until actually completed.  Changes in requirements will be handled by going through the Feature Driven Development process.

3.4 Monitoring and Controlling Mechanisms

The dotProject management application lets us enter details on tasks, then provides a list of tasks and their current completed percentage as updated by us.  It can also produce Gantt charts from that list of tasks, which will give us a visual representation of our schedule.
