8.0 Design Analysis

8.1 Alternatives

This section shall list all the design alternatives that your group has considered, based on the SRS. For example, the SRS may suggest one particular organization of features and modules, but your team may consider a different way to design and organize it. Consider this: The SRS may show one module responsible for initializing the whole system (which may be fine for the SRS). However, your design team may consider the alternative to have each of the independent modules perform its own respective initialization, rather than depend on some other global module to do the initializations. Be sure to list any and all design alternatives that your group may consider. (In the real world, usually the author of the SRS is not the same person as the software designer. That's why a SRS may suggest one design approach, but the design documents (like this one) may use a totally different one, depending on coding style, execution efficiency, maintainability, constraints imposed by the development language, etc.)

One significant design alternative that your group may consider is if the system should be approached using traditional Structured Design or Object-Oriented Design. Please note that if your team decides to use the OOD approach for the design documents (like this one), but traditional structured design was used for the SRS, then the organization of the modules in this document may be extremely different from the organization of the modules in the SRS. For example, in the SRS, each process may become essentially a module using Structured Design. However, collections of processes in an SRS may somehow be combined into a single class (using OOD). Do you consider each class to be functionally equivalent to the term "module"? If so, then be sure to describe each member function (routine) that comprises each module (class), so that all the processes get covered.

When doing OOD, most people arrive at different designs for the same type of system. Due to this common variance, please discuss each team member's candidate classes as design alternatives.
