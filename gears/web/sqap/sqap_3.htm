3.0 Documentation

3.1 Purpose

Briefly, the purpose of this documentation section of the SQAP is to identify the documents that will be written for the project. You need to distinguish between those documents that directly affect the quality of the end product and those that do not. Such documents (and their quality attributes) are items that need to be discussed here, in section 3 of the SQAP.
--JC.

3.2 Minimum Documentation Requirements

For this section, you need to identify the documentation related to development, verification, validation, use, and maintenance of the software. You also need to specify how documents are to be checked for adequacy, including the criteria to be applied and the related review or audit.

How do you do that? Well, first start with the list of documentation. Not all of the documents that you write for this project will be directly related to product quality. Those documents that DO affect the product quality get included in this section (3.2). Those other documents that do NOT affect the product quality get included in the next section (3.3). I would suggest that you consider at least the SQAP, SRS, SDD, Source Code, PPRs, STP, and the SUM as the documents with the most influence on the quality of your product.

Using that list, you then need to explain how you determine if the quality of each item is adequate. The way they are checked for adequacy is by having one (or more) persons on your team review the documentation written by somebody else. The general idea is that an extra set of eyes helps to find mistakes, and that each person tends to be blind to some of their own mistakes. Another quality check point is provided by the instructor. The instructor will also review the product documentation, providing feedback about sections that need to be improved. Another important thing to mention is that for the SRS, SDD, source code, and the STP, there will be formal inspection meetings (peer reviews). These activities are really key to promoting and improving quality in the product!
--JC.

3.3 Other

This section is just to identify all the other documents included in this project, which are not necessarily required to promote the quality of the product. Please don't think that these other documents are useless--on the contrary; they're very important. It's just that their purpose may not be primarily related to the quality of the product. For example, the weekly status reports are probably more related to schedule than quality. Another example might be the PMP, which deals more with schedule and costs, than quality.
--JC.
