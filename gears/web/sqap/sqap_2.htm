2.0 Management

2.1 Organization

In the real world, the SQA organization is separate from the development organization. Only product management is the organization that is over the SQA and Development organizations. Since we do not have a separate SQA group in the class, you need to state this deviation from the industry standard. As an alternative to the traditional organizational structure, explain that your team will implement QA practicies by each team member. You may want to say that a team vote will be used in places where quality decisions need to out-weigh development schedule decisions.
--JC.

2.2 Tasks and Responsibilities

Describe the portion of the software life cycle covered by the SQAP. Describe the SQA tasks and their sequence.

For example, when the RP is being developed, one SQA task is to ensure that it is as complete as possible for the customer to understand the proposed functionality of the product. Your job, as SQA, is to keep refining the RP until the customer is satisfied. If time runs out for this activity (i.e. you couldn't finish the RP in time), then the quality of this deliverable is probably less than optimal.

Another example is for the Specification phase of the software life cycle. At the end of this phase the SRS is produced. Due to the great importance for accuracy and completeness in this document, a Peer Review will be conducted. Each team member (and the instructor) will spend a week inspecting the product, recording all noticeable defects. At the peer review meeting, all defects will be reported and recorded. The moderator will assign a team member to close each outstanding item. This is all done to promote and improve the quality of this document.

When the implementation phase begins (CS525), we will do some code reviews also.

When the system testing begins (CS525), each build will be tested by the team members and test engineers. This testing activity is yet another task done during a software life cycle phase to promote and improve quality.

For each phase, try to identify tasks and responsibilities that will affect the quality of the product.
--JC.
