5.0 Reviews and Audits

5.1 Purpose

Anybody can say, "the quality of our team's SRS is sufficient," but how could an un-biased independent evaluator draw the same conclusion? Answer: By using the same standard to measure the item. The standards discussed in section 4, are used during reviews and audits to determine if the product does in fact posses sufficient quality. The purpose of section 5 of the SQAP is to explain how and when various reviews and audits will be done to measure a product's quality.
--JC.

5.2 Minimum Requirements

For each item listed in section 3.2, list when and by whom the item's quality will be assessed. You also need to identify which standards (from section 4.2) will be used to assess this quality.

For example, the SRS will have an internal audit among the team members to be sure that the document looks ready for the peer review. This internal audit use the documentation standards, as well as the instructions written in the SRS templates, to determine if the SRS is ready for distribution and review. After that, the SRS will go through a formal Ispection by each team member, including the instructor. The Requirements checklist provided by Steve McConnell will be used as a guideline. At the end of this formal inspection, there will be a formal meeting for all the team reviewers to discuss all defects found. The results of the inspection meeting will be followed up by the Moderator to ensure that every action item got closed.

That's just for the SRS. Think about all the other items and how they will be reviewed and audited.
--JC.

5.3 Other

As mentioned previously, the items listed in section 3.3 do not directly affect the end product's quality. However, these items do need to be reviewed and audited. Explain when that gets done, who does it, and what standards are used to determine if the items pass the review/audit.

For example, someone may collect status and update the Gantt chart for the week. However, a different person on the team will examine the latest Gantt chart to determine if a resonable amount of normal progress has been made this week. If it looks like the project is falling behind, then the Gantt chart may be in error, or the Gantt chart may be accurately reporting that the project really is falling behind. This is the kind of things that you want to discuss in this section.
--JC.
