6.0 Problem Reporting and Corrective Action

When errors are found in your work products, how will your team resolve them? Most teams feel that every person on the team is empowered to make any and all changes they feel are necessary to the products. However, occasionaly a person feels that somebody changed their work, making the product worse rather than better. When this happens, then perhaps a discussion with the whole team is in order. If that's the model your team plans to adopt, then so state.

Other teams would like to have one person solely responsible for all changes that occur to a given product. When using this model, if a teammate finds an error in a product, then the normal process is to send an e-mail to the product owner describing the suggested improvement. It is then up to the product owner to decide if the suggestion improves the product or not.

There are other methods that can be used--ranging from very informal to highly formal. You need to explain the process that your team will use for reporting, tracking, and resolving problems in the product and the process.

Note that when we finally start doing software builds in CS525, then we will use PPRs to report problems with the software. Be sure to explain the Problem Tracking Process used by PPRs in this section of the SQAP.
